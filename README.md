<h2>Ocean Warming</h2>
<p>
Oceans absorb more than 90% of the excess heat produced by global warming.
As seawater heats up, oxygen levels decrease, making it difficult for
marine animals to breathe and survive.
</p>
<p>
Ocean warming also disrupts food chains, increases coral bleaching,
and strengthens storms such as hurricanes. Small temperature changes
can lead to large ecosystem damage over time.
</p>
<div class="tag">🌍 Warmer oceans affect weather, fisheries, and biodiversity</div>
</section>

<h2>Ocean Acidification</h2>
<p>
As carbon dioxide levels rise in the atmosphere, oceans absorb more CO₂.
This forms carbonic acid in seawater, lowering the ocean’s pH and making
it more acidic.
</p>
<p>
Acidic water weakens coral reefs and shell‑forming organisms such as
plankton, clams, and oysters. Since plankton form the base of the marine
food web, acidification threatens the entire ecosystem.
</p>
<div class="tag">🔬 Chemical changes silently damage marine life</div>
</section>

<h2>Coral Bleaching</h2>
<p>
Coral reefs depend on algae that live inside them for food and energy.
When ocean temperatures become too high, corals expel these algae,
causing them to turn white — a process known as coral bleaching.
</p>
<p>
Repeated bleaching events reduce reef biodiversity and weaken natural
coastal defenses against waves and storms.
</p>
<div class="tag">🛡️ Coral reefs protect coastlines and support marine life</div>
</section>

<h2>Sea Level Rise</h2>
<p>
Global warming melts glaciers and ice sheets while warm seawater expands.
These processes cause sea levels to rise worldwide.
</p>
<p>
Rising seas flood coastal ecosystems, damage infrastructure,
and threaten homes, schools, and freshwater supplies in coastal cities
and island nations.
</p>
<div class="tag">🏠 Coastal populations face increasing flood risks</div>
</section>

<h2>Changing Fish Migration Routes</h2>
<p>
As ocean temperatures shift, many fish species migrate toward cooler waters.
This changes where fish are found and disrupts traditional fishing locations.
</p>
<p>
Some species are unable to adapt quickly, causing population decline,
which affects food supply, jobs, and marine balance.
</p>
<div class="tag">🎣 Climate change reshapes global fisheries</div>
</section>
